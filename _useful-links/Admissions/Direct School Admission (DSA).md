<h3><strong>Direct School Admission - Secondary 1 (2027) Intake</strong></h3>
<p>Primary 6 students with an interest and talent in (A) Sports and (B) Science,
Technology, Engineering &amp; Mathematics (STEM) areas can apply for Direct
School Admission (DSA) to Secondary 1 (2027) via the DSA exercise this
year.</p>

<h4><strong>(A)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; SPORTS</strong></h4>
<p><strong><u>Student-Athlete Programme</u></strong>
</p>
<p>The SKSS Student-Athlete Programme adopts a 6-prong approach aimed at
providing a supportive ecosystem for students to develop holistically in
sports, academic learning and other domains of growth.</p>
<p>Beyond playing the sport, as part of Educational Career Guidance, students
will be also exposed to sports-related knowledge, post-secondary courses
and career pathways.</p>
<p>Students can also consider opting for Exercise &amp; Sports Science as
a subject in their upper secondary years.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/SKSS_Student_Athlete_Programme.png">
</div>
<p><strong><u>Selection Criteria for Sports Talent Areas</u></strong>
</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/SKSS_SAP.jpg">
</div>
<p>Applicants should demonstrate a keen interest in sports but need not necessarily
have prior experience in the specific talent area they are applying for.
The school will assess applicants based on the following selection criteria*:</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Possesses general sports-related
fitness and physical ability</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Demonstrates Sport-specific technical
skills</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Exhibits good personal qualities</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Applicants are encouraged to include any participation in relevant CCAs
or external competition, which may include:</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; representation in zonal or national
competitions</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; being a member of the school team
or Junior Sports Academy (JSA)</p>
<h4><strong>(B) &nbsp;&nbsp; STEM</strong></h4>
<p><strong><u>SKYentist Programme<br></u></strong>
</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/DSA_2027_STEM.png">
</div>
<p>The Seng Kang Youth-Scientist (SKYentist) Programme aims to develop passionate
and talented students holistically in the areas of STEM beyond the mainstream
curriculum through SKSS’ two signature programmes, the Applied Learning
Programme (ALP) and Seng Kang Youth Learn Apply Breakthrough! (SKY L.A.B!)
talent development programme.</p>
<p>Through SKSS ‘Food for the Future’ ALP, all students explore issues and
develop solutions concerning Food Security and Sustainability. The ALP
curriculum involves various authentic experiences, such as soil-based and
hydroponics urban farming, food-related scientific investigations and solutioning
using coding and Artificial Intelligence (AI). SKYentists will also be
involved in projects that tackle food-related issues and explore alternative
food options.</p>
<p>The SKY L.A.B! programme develops SKYentists’ interest in research and
equip them with knowledge and skills in STEM through a progressive and
extensive 3-year spiral curriculum that includes workshops, learning journeys
and competitions. SKYentists capture their journey in the programme through
a digital portfolio that can be utilised for their future post-secondary
and career pursuits.</p>
<p><strong><u>Selection Criteria for STEM Talent Area</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants should demonstrate a keen interest in STEM-related areas but
need not necessarily have prior experience in the specific talent area
they are applying for. The school will assess applicants based on the following
selection criteria*:</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Demonstrates strong interest in
STEM subjects</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Demonstrates critical, problem-solving
and creative thinking.</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Exhibits good personal qualities</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Applicants are encouraged to include any participation in relevant CCAs
or external competition, which may include:</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; representation in STEM projects
and/or competitions, and/or participation in STEM activities and interest
groups</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>*Do note that meeting the selection criteria does not guarantee that the
applicant will be shortlisted/given an offer.</p>

<p><strong>Advisory to Parents &amp; Students</strong>
</p>
<p>Parents and students are encouraged to choose schools thoughtfully, considering
the student's aptitudes and strengths alongside each school's academic
and non-academic requirements and the programmes available to develop their
talents.</p>
<p>Students who are successfully admitted to the school via DSA are required
to honour their commitment to the school and participate in the activities
related to their selected talent area throughout their years at the school.</p>
